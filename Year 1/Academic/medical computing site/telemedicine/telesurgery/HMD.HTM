Telemedicine

Head Mounted Displays (HMDs)

Types of HMD

There are two broad categories of HMDs: low resolution and high resolution.

Low resolution HMDs use LCD displays (like those found on old laptops) and are unsuitable for medical application. They can only give resolutions of up to 240 TV lines.

High resolution displays, which use cathode ray tubes (like those found in TVs), are capable of resolutions of 1000 TV lines on display areas as small as 21 x 28 mm.

Creating images at slightly different angles for each eye gives a 3D image to the viewer.

However, they can only display monochrome images.





Colourizing monochrome images

The method which gave us colour television (spatial colour-pixel ) is unsuitable because with the panel being so close to the eye that the pixels are too large to convince a viewer that the image is really coloured.

The temporal field-sequential approach is much more effective. This simply involves switching between red, green and blue filters (in the shutter) at a high rate (about 180 Hz) giving the viewer the impression of real colour images (at a refresh rate of 60 Hz).

Other uses

They have many uses besides in medicine.

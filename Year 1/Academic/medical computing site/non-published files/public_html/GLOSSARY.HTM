Bandwidth: The rate at which data is transferred.

Telemedicine: The exchange of information at a distance whether the information is voice, an image, elements of a medical record, or commands to a surgical robot. It seems reasonable to think of telemedicine as the remote communication of information of information to facilitate clinical care.

Telepresence: When the operator perceives the remote site to be directly in front of him or her.

CT (computer tomography) scan: a cross-sectional view of an internal body structure using computerized axial tomography. Also known as CAT scan.

MRI scan: electronic images of specific atoms and molecular structures in solids, especially human cells, tissues, and organs using a nuclear magnetic resonance spectrometer.

ECG (Electrocardiogram): A curve traced out by an electrocardiograph which aids the detection and diagnosis of heart abnormalities. The data is obtained by measuring electrical potentials on the body surface and generating a record of the electrical currents associated with heart muscle activity

Asystole: failure of the ventricles of the heart to contract

ventricular ectopic beats: ventricle beats in abnormal parts of the body

atrial premature beats: atrium contractions occurring before expected

bradycardia: Slowness of the heart rate, usually fewer than 60 beats per minute in an adult human

tachycardia: A rapid heart rate, especially one above 100 beats per minute in an adult

Expert System: A program that uses available information, heuristics, and inference to suggest solutions to problems in a particular discipline

Radiology: The branch of medicine that deals with the use of radioactive substances in diagnosis and treatment of disease

Laparoscopy: An operation in which a slender tubular endoscope is inserted through an incision in the abdominal wall and used for viewing the abdominal or pelvic cavities

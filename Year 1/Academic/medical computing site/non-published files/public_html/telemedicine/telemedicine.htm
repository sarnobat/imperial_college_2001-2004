What is telemedicine?

Telemedicine is the use of electronic information and communications technologies to provide and support health care when distance separates the patients from the professionals.

Who is telemedicine of interest to?

Many professions are involved in the administration and planning of telemedicine. Here are the main ones:

  • Primary Care Practitioners

  • Billing/Insurance companies

  • Outreach Clinics

  • Grant Writers

  • Outcome Analysts

  • Continuing Education personnel

  • Engineers

  • Computing Specialists

  • Information Professionals

In what contexts of medicine might it be used?

There is a wide range of areas in which telemedicine is being used:

  • Primary Health Care

  • Monitoring and diagnosis

  • Consultancy

  • Education

  • Surgery

to name but a few.

What is the common ingredient in all telemedical applications?

In one word, the INTERNET is responsible for growth of telemedicine. Tasks which would have taken hours/days/weeks can now be accomplished in seconds all because of the speed of electrical conduction.

Do we really need telemedicine?

In a report carried out in 1997, it was estimated that 15.6m adults were searching for online health information (which was about 43% of all internet users at the time). The figure has been increasing by tens of millions every year since.

Medical facilities are not available with equal ease to all the population. Those:

  • living in remote areas

  • limited by travel or time costs

  • the elderly

are all awaiting the benefits of telemedicine’s evolution.

Where in the world is telemedicine being practised?

In 1998, it was reported that there were over 200 active programs throughout the world in countries such as:

  • Chile

  • India

  • Israel

  • Japan

  • Scotland

  • Taiwan

157 of the 200 were in rural areas of the USA.

Considering the advances in the fields mentioned above since 1998, the given figures are likely to have changed significantly.

(in 1993, there were only 10 active programs)
